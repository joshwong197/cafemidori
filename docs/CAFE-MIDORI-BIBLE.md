# Café Midori — Style Bible

Original-art reference for the Midori team's home app. Claude Code generates assets against **this document only**. No external character-art references, no Persona 5 / Atlus source material. Nods and archetype hints are welcome; cloned outfits, masks, or signature costume pieces are not.

---

## 1. The café itself

A small, warm, Showa-era-feel backstreet coffee-and-curry shop on the ground floor of an old narrow-gabled Tokyo building, with upstairs floors converted into private rooms for each Midori agent. Feels lived-in, not staged.

- **Palette**: warm wood browns, brass, lamp-amber, deep forest green booth leather, charcoal iron fittings. Cool greys + soft neon only for night exterior and the attic.
- **Light**: golden-hour bias indoors. Small pools of lamp light rather than flat overhead. Night scenes lean cool-blue with warm window glow.
- **Scale**: 32×32 character sprites, 4-direction walk cycles, 4 frames per direction. Room tiles 16×16. Camera: top-down with gentle isometric tilt (roughly 2:1 dimetric), NOT flat top-down.
- **Motion cadence**: idle animations 8 fps, walks 12 fps. Ambient loops long (8-20s) so the place doesn't feel jittery.
- **Mood**: you walk in and the place was already living without you.

**Café ground floor (shared)**:
- Counter with espresso machine, siphon coffee setup, small curry pot on a low flame
- Four booth tables along one wall, two two-seaters at the window
- Chalkboard menu (menu items TBD — original items, not the game's menu)
- Small TV on a wall shelf, analog clock
- Bookshelf near the stairs up

**Upstairs landing + attic stairs**: narrow wooden stairs; each agent's door off the landing.

## 3. Character design — original looks with archetype nods

Every agent is rendered as an original Midori character. **Nods are allowed** (e.g. glasses, a colour that favours red, hair in a particular colour family). **Costumes, masks, or signature outfit pieces from the source material are not to be reproduced**, even in abstracted pixel form. If you would need a reference image of the source character to draw it, you are too close — design from the description below instead.

All agents wear **casual modern work-appropriate clothing** — a small-team-office-in-Auckland feel, not uniforms and not costumes. No mix-and-match: the whole roster is in this register. (If we later do a "formal night" or "café uniform" set, it's a coordinated change for all ten, not per-character.)

---

### Joker — Ren (Chief Executive)
- Mid-height, slim build, dark unruly hair, calm face
- **Small nod**: round wire-frame glasses; dark grey-and-charcoal palette on clothes
- **Casual**: dark turtleneck or henley, dark trousers, apron (café duty). No tailcoat, no mask, no red gloves.
- **Prop**: espresso tamper or a ledger notebook

### Futaba — Sakura (Orchestrator)
- Short, petite, long orange hair in a loose side-plait or just down
- **Nod**: oversized round glasses (keep)
- **Casual**: hoodie (dark teal or olive), shorts or baggy jeans, sneakers, headphones around neck
- **Prop**: laptop, or a tablet showing a channel dashboard

### Haru — Okumura (Receptionist)
- Medium height, soft features, light-auburn curls in a short bob
- **Nod**: pastel pink accent somewhere (scarf or cardigan)
- **Casual**: cream blouse, long skirt, small silver watch, modest heels
- **Prop**: clipboard, small potted plant

### Yusuke — Kitagawa (Growth Lead)
- Tall, lean, dark navy-blue hair falling just past ears
- **Nod**: deep blue favoured in clothing
- **Casual**: navy button-down with sleeves rolled, paint-flecked canvas trousers, plain canvas shoes
- **Prop**: paintbrush tucked behind ear, a small sketchbook

### Kasumi — Yoshizawa (Credit Analyst)
- Athletic build, crimson-red hair in a low ponytail
- **Nod**: a single red hair ribbon
- **Casual**: burgundy sweater over a white collar, dark trousers, clean trainers
- **Prop**: colour-coded folder of scorecards

### Ryuji — Sakamoto (Account Manager)
- Tall, athletic, sandy-blond short hair, small scar hint on eyebrow
- **Nod**: a yellow accent (wristband or a stripe on his jacket)
- **Casual**: open track-style jacket over a plain t-shirt, jeans, running shoes
- **Prop**: call-script card, gym towel on shoulder sometimes

### Morgana — Mona (Portfolio Monitor)
- A small cat. Original design — NOT the source-material's cat. Medium-short fur, slim build, bright intelligent eyes.
- **Nod**: mostly black coat with a white chest/paws bib; no yellow neckerchief, no oversized head (avoid source-cat silhouette). Proportions are realistic-stylised cat, not mascot-cat.
- **Props / behaviour**: prefers the windowsill of the portfolio-map room; walks the café counter at idle; sits on stacked ledgers at Joker's counter sometimes.
- Talks — animated with a small mouth-move frame during speech and a tail-flick idle. No human clothes.

### Anne — Takamaki (Recovery Specialist)
- Tall, long blonde hair in twin tails (keep the twin-tail nod — it's a silhouette signature, not a costume)
- **Nod**: red favoured throughout her palette (jumper, jacket, accent)
- **Casual**: red wool jumper or a red jacket over cream, dark slim jeans, ankle boots
- **Prop**: a small hair clip she fidgets with, or a tea mug. *(No catsuit, no cat-ears mask, no whip — those are costume.)*

### Makoto — Niijima (Compliance Officer)
- Medium height, neat chestnut-brown bob
- **Nod**: crimson headband or a crimson collar-tie
- **Casual**: fitted grey blazer over a plain shirt, slim black trousers, sensible flats
- **Prop**: policy binder, fountain pen

### Akechi — (Legal Counsel, On Call)
- Medium-tall, slim, tidy light-brown hair slightly longer than collar
- **Nod**: ochre-mustard favoured (scarf, or jacket trim). A coffee cup is always near him.
- **Casual**: camel overcoat over a charcoal shirt, dark trousers, polished shoes
- **Prop**: a single case file, reading glasses on a chain

## 4. Animation set per agent

Each agent needs the following sprite sheets:
- **Idle** (4 frames, facing camera) — subtle breathing / small tic
- **Walk** (4 directions × 4 frames each)
- **Sit** (3 poses: at a booth, at their desk in their room, at the counter)
- **Interact** (1-2 context-specific frames: Joker pulls espresso; Yusuke paints; Anne swaps wardrobe; Futaba types; etc.)
- **Talk** (2 frames, small mouth/expression change — used in chat scenes)

Plus one **portrait frame** per agent (larger bust-up, used in the genome viewer and room headers).

## 5. Cafe ambient scenes (scripted loops, not per-user state)

- Joker at the counter wiping a cup
- Kasumi at a window booth with a folder open
- Futaba on the staircase with headphones on, tapping a laptop
- Morgana (the cat) on the windowsill, or padding across the counter, or curled on the ledger stack
- Yusuke sketching at a back booth
- Rain on the window at evening; lamps warm up
- Day-mode: bright window wash, quieter ambient track, morning coffee routine. Evening-mode: warm lamp pools, soft rain/city ambient, curry pot simmering.
- No extra NPCs — Morgana is the only cat; the café crowd is the team itself.

Loops run independently — the cafe feels alive regardless of whether Joshua is speaking to anyone.

## 6. What Claude Code must NEVER generate

- Any costume piece taken from the source material (tailcoats, harlequin masks, skull masks, cat-ear masks, catsuit with tail, skeletal-pattern leggings, Fox's white fencing outfit, et cetera).
- Any named location from the source material (Mementos, the Metaverse, Palaces as depicted in the source).
- Any Atlus/Sega logos or trademark indicia.
- Any NPCs from the source material (Sojiro, Morgana-as-game-mascot, Igor, Lavenza, etc.).

If a requested prompt would require one of these, Claude Code should refuse the prompt and flag it to Joker for redirection.

## 7. What Claude Code SHOULD draw from

- This style bible, exclusively, for character designs and café layout.
- General Showa-era Tokyo café aesthetic references (public-domain photography, period-consistent interior design) for background atmosphere only.
- Generic pixel-art conventions (Stardew Valley-class readability at 32×32, Ib-class mood-lighting discipline) as grammar, not as copy.
